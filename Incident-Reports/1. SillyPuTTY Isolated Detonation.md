# SillyPuTTy Incident Report 

### Date: May 6, 2026
### Contained (Isolated Environment)

## Executive Summary
On May 5th, a dynamic detonation was performed on a suspicious binary identified as putty.exe. The ananlysis was conducted in a fully network isolated environment. The Telemetry confirms the sample was a Remote Access Trojan that utilizes decoy tactics, PowerShell execution, and non-standard port callbacks for Command & control (C2).

## Artificat Metadata 

**File Name**
  - putty[.]exe

**SHA256**
  - 0c82e654ce9c8fdgfdf4899718efa3767e974c9eec5a8fc18a167f93cea6ee83

**Packing Status**
  - Confirmed that is was a Packed Binary

**Decoy**
  - Launched a "PuTTy Configuration" GUI to mask the background activity.

## Host-Base Indicators

**Execution Flow (Event 1)**
  - The malware attempted to resolve github.com and bonus.corporatebonusapplication.local.

**Behavioral Obserbation**
  - A tansient blue windows (CLI) was observed immediately after detonation, correlating with the Sysmon process creation event.

**Persistence (Event 13)**
  - The sample demonstrated the capability to modify the Windows Registry.

## Network Based Indicators

**DNS Resolution (Event 22)**
  - The malware attempted to resolve `github.com` and `bonus2.corporatebonusapplication.local`,

**Interception**
  - All DNS requests were successfully sinkholed to `127.0.0.1` via FakeNet-NG.

**C2 Callback (Event 3)**
  - Identified an outbound TCP connection attempt to `7.74.181.33` on Port `22`.

**Tactical Note**
  - The use of Port 22 for C2 traffic suggests an attempt to bypass egress filtering by masquerading as encrypted SSH administrative traffic.
